Shape n' Swarm
Actuated Experience Lab
Tangibly authoring swarm robots using LLMs.
Video
Paper Abstract
This paper introduces an interactive system for authoring Swarm UIs—interactive clusters of tabletop robots—by allowing users to control the robots through tangible manipulation and voice commands. Inspired by grounded visions in programmable interactive material, we define this approach as ‘Manipulation-Aware Generative Actuation.' The system employs multiple code-generating LLM agents, based on Chat-GPT 4o, which are architected to work together to take user inputs to process into three major generative tasks: 1. thematically interpret the shape created by users, 2. create animations to the manipulated shape, and 3. flexibly build and map I/O within the cluster of robots for creating interaction. With our proof-of-concept prototype that uses toio robots, we ran a user study with 11 participants, and they were able to flexibly and easily create diverse physical actuation and interaction without any coding. To lead this novel research space, we also share limitations, research challenges, and design recommendations.
TorqueCapsules++
Sony Research x Actuated Experience Lab
Enabling directional haptics through flywheel-based devices.
Demo
Current Work
I am working on a 3D-visualiation and physics modelling tool that allows users to visualize and calculate torque-induced haptics. This includes building an algorithm to automatically suggest flywheel placement, orientation, and spin speed based on the specified haptic feeling. I am also prototyping a new TorqueCapsule form factor with a smaller motor and a more compact design.
